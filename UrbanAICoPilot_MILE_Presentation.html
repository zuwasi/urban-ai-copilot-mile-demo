What Makes MILE Reconstructable

  • Explicit state-space modelRecurrent memory ht, stochastic latent state st, posterior/prior moments — all directly expressible in Wolfram Language.
  • Closed-form objectiveThe ELBO decomposes into action likelihood, BEV likelihood, observation likelihood, and a diagonal Gaussian KL — every term is computable.
  • Published ablationsResolution sweep, latent dimension, and recurrent vs. reset-state frequency tradeoffs provide ground truth for validation.
  • Camera-first architectureNo LiDAR dependency makes MILE directly relevant to cost-sensitive automotive deployments — exactly the market ESL serves.

Paper at a Glance

Mathematical Core

Latent model from the paper

The MILE generative model is reconstructed equation-by-equation in Wolfram Language. Every term is executable and audited.

Generative Structure (Wayve MILE)

h1 ~ δ(0) — zero-initialized recurrent memory
s1 ~ Normal(0, I) — initial stochastic latent state
ht+1 = fθ(ht, st) — deterministic recurrent update
st+1 ~ Normal(μθ(ht+1, at), σθ(ht+1, at) · I) — action-conditioned prior
These equations map directly to Wayve's MILE paper §3. The action-conditioning on at is what makes the prior predictive rather than autoregressive.

Imagination Mode

Observe vs. imagine

MILE's observation dropout mechanism is a key innovation: the model can predict future states without sensor input, enabling proactive planning through "imagination."

Why This Matters for Automotive

  • Sensor failure resilienceIf cameras are temporarily occluded, the world model can continue predicting — degraded but continuous.
  • Quantified degradationThe audit verifies monotone uncertainty increase: observe-heavy 0.223 → balanced 0.231 → imagine-heavy 0.252.
  • Product gatingAn OEM can use the uncertainty signal to trigger graceful handoff to the driver when imagination-mode confidence drops.
From Wayve's paper: Observation dropout probability p controls the balance. Mean imagination streak = p/(1−p). This is derived in our proof appendix and verified in the audit.
Safety Layer

Safety Supervisor

The ESL-added safety layer implements deterministic, auditable intervention rules applied after the learned model's trajectory proposal — required for L2+/L3 certification.

Intervention Rules

  • Red-light stopIf SignalState == "Red" and path crosses stop line → y-coordinates clipped, speed collapsed to minimum-risk.
  • Obstacle bufferIf MinimumDistance < SafetyMarginMeters → lateral path shift + speed scaled to 0.55×.
  • Transparent trade-offRisk drops but reward may fall — the supervisor is intentionally conservative, and this is visible in the metrics.
